# 🎓 Campus Connect

### AI-Powered Study Group Matchmaking Platform

> A Product Management case study exploring how AI-assisted matching can improve academic collaboration by helping university students discover compatible study partners based on courses, schedules, learning pace, and study goals.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Campus Connect addresses a common problem faced by university students: academic collaboration is fragmented across WhatsApp groups, Telegram channels, Discord servers, and university portals. Finding reliable study partners is often time-consuming and inconsistent.

Instead of building another messaging platform, Campus Connect focuses on solving one high-value problem:

> **Helping students find the right study group at the right time.**

The MVP validates this hypothesis using a **Wizard of Oz** approach before investing in AI automation.

---

## 🚨 Problem Statement

Students struggle to organize collaborative learning because:

* Study opportunities are scattered across multiple communication platforms.
* Forming study groups requires manually messaging strangers.
* Existing LMS platforms support courses but not peer discovery.
* Generic messaging apps lack intelligent academic matching.
* Poorly matched groups reduce participation and motivation.

---

## 💡 Proposed Solution

Campus Connect is an AI-assisted platform that intelligently matches students into small, balanced study groups using:

* Course enrolled
* Subject confidence level
* Study goals
* Availability
* Learning pace

Rather than immediately building a complex AI system, the MVP validates demand using a **Wizard of Oz** prototype where matching is performed manually behind the scenes while preserving the intended user experience.

---

## 👩‍💼 About this Project

Campus Connect was created as an end-to-end Product Management case study to demonstrate product discovery, MVP planning, experimentation strategy, AI-assisted product design, and user-centered thinking.

The objective is not to showcase engineering complexity, but to illustrate how product managers identify problems, validate assumptions, prioritize features, and design measurable MVPs before investing in development.
